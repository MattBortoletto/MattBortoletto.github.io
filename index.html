Matteo Bortoletto

About

I am a PhD Student in the Collaborative AI group at the University of Stuttgart, Germany, advised by Prof. Andreas Bulling. My core research lies at the intersection of artificial and social intelligence.

During my PhD, I've also spent some time as a Visiting Graduate Scholar at the SCAI Lab, Johns Hopkins University, advised by Prof. Tianmin Shu.

Research Interests

My research focuses on how AI can understand others' mental states such as desires, beliefs, and intentions ‚Äî a concept known as theory of mind ‚Äî and how this understanding can improve interactions between humans and AI. In particular, I work on:
  • Designing and assessing AI models for theory of mind tasks that span multiple modalities, including vision and natural language
  • Probing and manipulating models' internal representations of mental states
  • Stress-testing theory of mind on large foundation models
  • Using theory of mind for planning
